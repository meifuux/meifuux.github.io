How to balance the business needs while ensure happy customer is a key.

Research

Observation & Interviews

To start with, I had research on the claw player user with Naturalistic Observation, one of the essential ways of getting passive data collection. I step a side and view claw players’ action & emotion. Follow up with a semi-structured interview with them. 

Some of the main findings are : 

Some claim the setting of the game centre is a deceive. They will rather purchase the prize online. This created a negative image for the branding and not a good praise to maintain long-term business. 

They start feel negatively after ~5 times playing in general. They feel disappointed when had already pay lots and didn’t get any back.

Players willing to get more tries for prize they are interested. On the same time, they will measure the prize’s value into Qualitative & Quantitative.

When asking of what they had experience during time in game centre, some mentioned about the payment method is inconvenient, they worry their machine will get played by others when they are lining up for recharge the member card.

They also feel anxious when they had gain the prize and will need to look for shop keepers for help.

I present the prototype for game players to have feedback for the design. Users were encouraged to think aloud during the process. Evaluate

Usability Testing

For evaluate the effectiveness of the design, usability test is conducted with the claw game player. After introduce the project and some demographic questions (age range, level for experience in claw gaming, etc.) The following tasks are asked to completed by them:

  • Task 1 : Select the beginner level machine. Review the tips video of it.
  • Task 2 : Recharge your membership. Select master card for the payment method.
  • Task 3 : Hold the specific machine from Task 1. And after 5s release it.
  • Task 4 : Share you option on the machine to the community.

I asked them about the overall experience using the app, how effective they think of the solution, would they recommended this solution. I also let them complete the System Usability Scale (SUS) for a quantify result. 

Overall, the users liked the design. The design was given 82.95 SUS score, much higher than the average SUS score (68). They specially liked the solution because : 

  • It will offer needly help when don’t know how to claw.
  • The application is simple and easy to use.
  • E-payment and machine holding is convent and time saving.
  • Discount storage is great that they won’t forget / lost it again.
