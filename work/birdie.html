Birdie Mobile
Reform

Birdie is a telecommunications service provider in Hong Kong that offers its services via a mobile application. Unfortunately, the app's current layout is unfriendly and negatively impacts the user experience. This project aims to improve the app's interface and provide business recommendations to enhance the overall user experience.
DELIVERABLE
Telecommunications Provider display interface
SETTING

TIMELINE

Problem

How can we improve the not conducive to ease of UI and create positive experiences for customers?

My Solution

Restructure the architecture of the app and make the app more focus on the key-feature of the app.

Optimizing Design

Revamp the interface for better organization and ease of use, featuring renamed labels and reorganized structure. Allow users to reach desired features quickly with minimal clicks.

Efficient
Family Solution

Introducing Birdie Mobile with an all-in-one display and payment solution for families, with efficient features. A family plan with discounts may also be offered as a marketing incentive.

Stay Updated

Never miss a discount, billing, or event again with our user-friendly app featuring pop-up notifications and clear UI. Stay on top of important updates with reminders for unread items.

Research

Conducted a user research study on the Birdie Mobile app via surveys and interviews to gather feedback and identify areas for improvement. Made recommendations to enhance the user experience and contribute to ongoing app development.
42.9% of our participants were 18-34 years old and 42.8% were under 18 years old. We found that almost all of them had poor experience and missed ‘Birdie Friday’ which is one of the key feature value by customers. Participants said they were willing to play Birdie’s mini-game in order get free data, but a vast majority also cited layout of the app is difficult to access.
To accurately identify the problem, I have gathered a group of users to complete several tasks and recorded the time taken for each task.

Research Conclusions

Based on the results of the interview and research, we can conclude the pain points that need to be addressed.
These apps should be fast and convincing, with e-payment options for remote ticketing.
Users value time-saving, security, and FaceID, but dislike one-time passwords via SMS.
It is important for users to easily access key information. (5s rules!)
