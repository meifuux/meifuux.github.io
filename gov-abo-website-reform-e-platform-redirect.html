Design outcome

ABO had requested to work on 2 items for this project, the official website & a new e-platform. The 2 platform is inter-related but still 2 difference target.

Official website

Website Reform

Target users :
Hong Kong citizens

Usage :
General informative page display all the event available for the public.
Introduce LCSD / ABO this department to public.

VS

E-platform

New all-in-one booking system for teachers

‍Target users :
Hong Kong local schools & teacher
‍
Usage :
Inform, book, remark suitable event for students which only can apply by schools.

CMS should be included to control the update & modification of news & activities. Therefore, the design should be reusable.

I was introduced during the middle of the project. In which the previous designer had a brief draft of the e-platform. However, the client is not satisfied with the outcome.

Check on Final Design

Research

Competitive analysis

I had also dig-in for competitive analysis for inspiration how others handle huge data. I had a reference on the website of banks, event booking pages : Timeout.com.hk, LCSD official site, Klook, Timeable.

In summary, there are lots that could be introduced & update on the site.

  • A calendar is a good method to display clear which date / what event having.
  • Multiple layer navigation/ Mega menu is adopted for such a large amount of info.
  • A clear detailed filter / category could help users filter out suitable event for them. This encourage more chance citizen to join activities which suit user.
More details

Review Original - Submitted design & ABO Original website

I had study on the pervious designer's design & ABO's original website design. I had figure out many item are missing & the submitted design is still kinda rough.

information architecture

To start, I dig in to organise an overview of the structure of ABO’s website, a sitemap is then created. 

Review full table

It’s noted the structure of the site is huge. With most of the information structured layer-by-layer. Some pages are duplicated that could be remove. This map could also help to check if things had been missing for both me, as a UX/UI designer & the technical team.
‍
Based on this sitemap, I had reconstruct the information architecture for both platform creation later.

I had combine some of the items and rename them. For example, “檔案室(過往節目)” to “過往節目”. Simplify the structure, helps reduce users abandon the site and move on. This will be run counter to the purpose - Attract more citizens to join LCSD’s activities.
‍
After organisation, it's noted that we can separated 2 types of scheme display for website.
a) Scheme pages - Having multiple sub-pages & category for activities under the scheme (e.g. 學校文化日)
b) Scheme pages - No sub-pages for activities (e.g. scheme under 社區拓展計劃)

While for E-platform: 4 scheme are displayed but only AOA (天空故事) is a different layout.

Design

Design alternatives

Based on the research & client requirements, when design the platforms following should be keep in mind.

Design Guide

I had created a general design guide for the development make sure all the structure are synchronise.

E-platform - greenish blue
For e-platform, I chosen a greenish blue color as primary color. It represents woods & nature gives out a feeling of soothing, relaxing, and youthful. While Blue represents both the sky and the sea. It often symbolizes serenity, stability, wisdom, calming color. Which fits the image of the teacher, the target users.

Official Website - Orange
In contrast to the e-platform, orange is chosen fits the branding ofthe Leisure and Cultural Services Department. Orange color calls to mind feelings of excitement, enthusiasm. Which fits the nature of ABO department.

Final design Prototype

During the whole design process, I had paid special attention to action and feedback. Which I noticed missing from the original design. It is very important to always keep the user informed, providing them with the right feedback, at the right time. To reduce users’ confusion & anxiety.

ABO Website Reform

1. Homepage

  • A side-navigation bar is set up with all the button for sub-pages.
  • On right-side having a calendar to display all event.
  • Centre have all the main promotion.

2. About us

  • The design aim to summarise content in tabs.

3. Event scheme page (With sub-pages / Category)

  • 1st page : Scheme introduction - Description scheme objective etc.
  • 2nd page : Year's scheme - Describe the scheme of the year (e.g. fee, application method). To navigate quickly a category & year filter is shown on right.
  • 3nd page : Scheme activity summary - All activity under the scheme & category.
    4th page : Some category with description, a pop up will shown when hover.
  • 5th page : Activity page - Activity details & their sub-activities. "Join now" button had been located on the bottom.

4. Event scheme page (Without sub-pages / Category)

  • 1st page : Scheme introduction - Description scheme objective etc.
  • 2nd page : Activity summary of the year - All activity of that year are shown.
  • 3rd page : Activity page - Activity details.

5. Other activity

  • Similar to about us page, other activities are summarised into 1 page.

6. Archive

  • Archive page display all the past activity historys.
  • With clear year filter & activity category drop down list.

7. LSCD Links

  • Link summary list of LSCD. The design abandoned original sub-page display to a direct pop-up.

E-platform Deisgn

1. Homepage

  • A side-navigation bar - New feature is added requested by client.
  • On right-side having a calendar to display all event.
  • E-platform will only have 4 scheme been display. Client ask to change as images for their implementation in the future.

2. Regular scheme

  • Client-side agree to remove some of the complex / unnecessary content, to simple the layout.
  • Activity section table are shown when wish to book a venue. Filter are applied for age gruop filtering.

3. Regular scheme - Application form filling

  • After selecting event sections, forms are required to fill by interested teachers. (e.g. no. of participants)
  • The form is further simplified according to clients. (e.g. Agreement confirm page convert to link when clicked)
  • The form is converted the printable form to a digital-online form.

5. My application

  • A page record all application status & history. User can edit their application when needed.

4. AOA - Special combination scheme

  • AOA is originally is redirect to page hosted by Fong Fong Project d' Art 芳芳劇藝. Client would like to get this scheme all-in- one to E-platform.
  • AOA is a online scheme promote radio drama, therefore, most of the resource of this scheme are posted online.

5. Feedback

  • This page is applied for beta-testing to let user test on the prototype.

Evaluate

Usability Testing

After minor modifications, our technical teams had updated the functional prototype. Thanks to the client. They had invited real users (local school teachers) in testing out the system. 
The clients commented on mainly the technical issue & some minor alignment of functional prototype. For example excuse unnecessary fields for the booking system. Some minor comments had also been made for the design, like color schemes & navigation bar placement. Overall, this layout had been approved by the client & real customers.

Items to continue with : 

  • Technical issues with the system (CMS consummate)
  • Front-end alignment of spacing
